Getting Started

Follow these steps the first time you open the app.

Signing In
  • 1
    Open the App

    Launch Precision IoT on your mobile device. The login screen appears with two sign-in options.

  • 2
    Sign In with Google (recommended)

    Tap Sign in with Google. Choose your Google account from the picker. Your name and photo load automatically.

  • 3
    — or — Sign In with Email & Password

    Tap Sign in with Email, enter your registered email and password, then tap Sign In. Contact your administrator if you need an account.

  • 4
    Automatic Redirect to Dashboard

    On success you are taken straight to the Dashboard. Your profile is synced in the background.

ℹ️ New user?Your first Google Sign-In automatically creates an account — no extra registration needed.
⚠️ Blocked accountIf your account has been suspended you will see an Access Denied screen instead of the Dashboard. Contact your administrator to appeal.
📊

Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is the central hub of the app. Two tabs sit at the top; a profile icon is in the header.

🖥️ Devices Tab
Lists all your connected Raspberry Pi sensor devices. Claim new devices, view live status, and manage sensors or cameras.
🚨 Alerts Tab
Displays the real-time ML Intelligence Feed. Review, rate, and manage automated detection alerts.
📡 Network Health
At the bottom of the Devices tab — global latency meter and live bar chart of connectivity across your device fleet.
🔔 Push Notifications
Critical alerts are pushed to your device instantly, even when the app is in the background.
🖥️

Managing Devices

Adding (Claiming) a Device
  • 1
    Tap “+ Add Device”

    Button at the top of the Devices tab. Opens the list of unclaimed devices on the network.

  • 2
    Select a Device

    Tap the device you want to claim. Only devices not currently owned by another user appear here.

  • 3
    Confirm Claim

    Confirm in the dialog. The device is now yours and appears in your list.

Sensor Control

Tap SENSORS on any device card to open that device’s sensor management panel.

Available Operations
ActionHow ToEffect
View sensorsOpen the SENSORS panelLists every sensor with its label, GPIO pin, and ON/OFF state
Toggle ON / OFFTap the ON or OFF button next to a sensorSends a remote command to enable or disable that sensor on the device
Add sensorEnter label and GPIO pin number, tap AddRegisters a new sensor; the device picks it up on next poll
Delete sensorTap the delete icon on a sensor rowRemoves sensor from the system (requires confirmation)
ℹ️ Data refresh rateLive temperature and humidity readings poll every 15 seconds (DHT11 sensor). A toggle may take up to one cycle to propagate.
⚠️ Access requiredYou can only control sensors on devices you own or have been granted controller-level access to by an administrator.

Live Camera Streaming

Each Raspberry Pi device can stream live video directly into the app using HLS (HTTP Live Streaming).

Starting a Live Stream
  • 1
    Tap CAMERA on a Device Card

    The in-app HLS video player opens full-screen automatically.

  • 2
    Stream Buffers and Plays

    A loading indicator appears while the stream buffers. Playback begins automatically — no extra tap needed.

  • 3
    Continuous Loop

    The stream loops so you always see the latest footage without manually restarting.

  • 4
    Close the Player

    Tap the ✕ close button (top-right) to exit and return to the Devices tab.

What the Player Shows
ItemDetails
Device LabelShown at top (e.g., “📹 Front Gate — Live Stream”)
Loading SpinnerVisible while stream buffers; disappears when live
Status MessagesConnecting…, Stream ready, or error text shown inline
Auto RetryPlayer reconnects automatically on transient network failures
⚠️ Device must be ACTIVEThe stream will not load if the device shows OFFLINE. Verify the Raspberry Pi is powered on and connected to the internet.

ML Alerts & Ratings

The Alerts tab shows a live feed of events detected by the machine-learning model running on your sensor devices.

Alert Retention Settings

Choose how long alerts are kept before automatic deletion. Tap your preferred period at the top of the Alerts tab:

7 days 15 days 30 days Never

Your selection saves automatically. “30 days” is the default. Use Never only for critical records.

Reading an Alert Card
ComponentDescription
Risk BadgeCRITICAL HIGH MEDIUM LOW
Device NameWhich sensor device triggered the alert
Detected ObjectsWhat the ML model found (e.g., “person”, “vehicle”, “fire”)
ConfidenceModel certainty as a percentage (e.g., “92.0% CONFIDENCE”)
Preview ImageThumbnail captured at the moment of detection (tap to enlarge)
TimestampRelative time since alert (e.g., “3 min ago”)
Accuracy Status✓ Accurate or ✗ Inaccurate once rated
Rating⭐ your 1–10 score (shown after rating)
Risk Level Colour Guide
🔴 CRITICAL — Immediate action required
🟠 HIGH — Prompt attention needed
🟡 MEDIUM — Review when possible
🔵 LOW — Informational only
Rating an Alert (Feedback)
  • 1
    Tap an Alert Card

    Opens the Rating dialog for that alert.

  • 2
    Mark Accuracy

    Tap Accurate if the detection was correct, or Inaccurate for a false positive.

  • 3
    Select a Score (1–10)

    Tap a number to rate the usefulness of the detection. 10 = very useful.

  • 4
    Tap OK / Submit

    Saved instantly. Card background updates to green (accurate) or red (inaccurate).

✅ Why rate alerts?Every rating is used to retrain the ML pipeline. Consistent feedback leads to fewer false alarms within days.
Viewing Full Alert Details

Tap VIEW DETAILS on any alert card to open a modal with:

  • Full-resolution capture image from the moment of detection
  • Complete detection metadata: detected classes, confidence, bounding boxes
  • Original risk level assigned by the model

Push Notifications

The app pushes a notification to your device the moment a new ML alert is generated, even when the app is not open.

Enabling Notifications
  • 1
    Allow on First Launch

    The app requests notification permission on sign-in. Tap Allow.

  • 2
    Re-enable if Denied

    Go to phone Settings → Apps → Precision IoT → Notifications and turn them on.

Notification Format
PartExample
Title🤖 CRITICAL
Bodyperson, vehicle — raspberrypi-front-gate
Sound & VibrationEnabled by default for all risk levels
Tap actionOpens the app on the Alerts tab
ℹ️ No duplicatesEach alert triggers exactly one notification, even if the app is reopened multiple times.

Profile & Sign Out

Viewing Your Profile

Tap the profile icon in the top-right of the Dashboard to open your profile panel. It shows your display name, profile photo, registered email, and sign-in method.

Signing Out
  • 1
    Open Profile Panel

    Tap the profile icon in the top-right corner.

  • 2
    Tap Sign Out

    Confirm in the dialog. Session is cleared and the app returns to the Login screen.

⚠️ Your data is safeSigning out does not delete devices or alert history. Everything is restored on next sign-in.

Troubleshooting

Sign-in fails or loops
  • Ensure a stable internet connection.
  • Use the correct Google account (the one with access).
  • Close and reopen the app, then try again.
  • For email/password: check caps lock and trailing spaces.
Device shows OFFLINE
  • Confirm the Raspberry Pi is powered on.
  • Verify Wi-Fi / internet connectivity on the device.
  • Restart the device and wait 30 seconds to reconnect.
  • If persistent, contact your system administrator.
Camera stream won’t load
  • Confirm the device status is ACTIVE.
  • Check your own internet connection.
  • Close and reopen the player — it retries automatically.
  • Verify no firewall rule is blocking the stream URL.
Sensor toggle has no effect
  • Confirm the device is online.
  • Wait one poll cycle (~15 seconds) for state to propagate.
  • Restart the device if state remains stuck.
  • Make sure you have controller-level access to this device.
Push notifications not arriving
  • Go to phone Settings → Notifications → Precision IoT and allow notifications.
  • Sign out and back in to re-register your push token.
  • Disable Battery Saver mode that may block background activity.
Alerts feed is empty
  • Check your Alert Retention setting — old alerts may have been auto-deleted.
  • Verify at least one device is online and the ML service is running.
  • Pull down to refresh the Alerts tab.
